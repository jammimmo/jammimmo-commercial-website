How much is your apartment in Mermoz, your villa in the Almadies or your plot on the outskirts of Dakar worth? Estimating well means avoiding both **under-selling** and stalling the sale with a price that is **too high**. Here is the honest method.

## The right method: comparable properties

A property's value cannot be read from a universal grid: it is derived from **comparable properties** actually present on the market. You compare your property with listings of the same **type**, the same **neighbourhood** (or a nearby one), and a similar **surface area**, to draw out a **price range per m²**.

This is exactly what Jamm's valuation tool does: it relies on **real, verified listings** from its catalogue — never on an invented price. When no sufficient comparable exists, it does not fabricate a figure: an expert refines it for free within 24 h.

## The factors that drive the price in Dakar

- **Location**: this is the primary criterion. The gap between the upmarket Almadies and an outlying neighbourhood is considerable.
- **Surface area** and layout (number of bedrooms, rooms).
- **Condition and standing**: new, good condition, to be renovated.
- **The floor, orientation, view** (sea, open outlook).
- **Title security**: a property under a **land title** sells better and faster than one with an uncertain status.
- **Rental demand** in the area, which supports investment value.

## The pitfalls to avoid

- Relying on a **generic price/m² grid**: Dakar is too heterogeneous for that.
- Estimating "on emotion" or on the original purchase price.
- Ignoring the **acquisition costs** on the buyer's side (see our [acquisition costs guide](/guides/frais-acquisition-senegal)), which weigh on the total budget and therefore on the negotiation.
